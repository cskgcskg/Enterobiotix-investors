§ 01

The investors

i.

Thairm Bio

Scotland-based Life Sciences Trust · Most active lead

A Scotland-based investment trust / family office founded in 2012, headquartered in Edinburgh. Invests in early-stage life science companies; Mark R. Bamforth has spoken publicly on the company's microbiome supply-chain thesis. No public corporate site — profile linked via Crunchbase.

  • Apr 2026Co-led £19m financing for Phase 2b RISE trial
  • Apr 2024Backed £27m round (Phase 2 advancement)
  • Sep 2021Led $21.5m Series A
Crunchbase profile →
ii.

Scottish National Investment Bank

Public Investment Bank · Patient capital

Scotland's first development investment bank, wholly owned by the Scottish Government and capitalised with a £2bn ten-year commitment. Co-led the latest round; contributed £6m to the 2024 raise alongside other equity.

  • Apr 2026Co-led £19m round with Thairm Bio
  • Apr 2024£6m contribution within £27m round
Visit SNIB →
iii.

Kineticos Life Sciences / Ventures

US Life Sciences Investor · Strategic

North Carolina-based life sciences consulting firm and venture capital company. Kineticos Ventures (the VC arm, led by President Frank Lis) joined as a new investor at Series A and continued into the 2024 round.

  • Apr 2024Participated in £27m round
  • Sep 2021Joined $21.5m Series A as new US investor
Visit Kineticos →
iv.

Scottish Enterprise

Public Economic Development Investor · Existing

Scotland's national economic development agency, with a Growth Equity arm that has been an existing investor across multiple EnteroBiotix rounds since the early stages.

  • Apr 2024Existing investor in £27m round
  • Sep 2021Existing investor in $21.5m Series A
  • 2019 →Earlier-round backer
Visit Scottish Enterprise →
v.

SIS Ventures

Mission-Aligned Investor · Existing

Wholly-owned subsidiary of Social Investment Scotland, FCA-authorised and launched in 2018. Invested in EnteroBiotix's £2m round in 2019 and the Series A in 2021, with a focus on locking the social mission into the company's articles.

  • Sep 2021Existing investor in $21.5m Series A
  • 2019Backed earlier £2m oversubscribed round
